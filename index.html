The double-slit experiment

wave · particle · the observer

Fire light (or any quantum particle) at a barrier with two narrow slits and watch where it lands on the screen behind. The result is the strangest in all of physics — and you can drive every part of it here.

The three modes

  • Wave — treat the light as a continuous wave. It goes through both slits and the two ripples interfere, painting bright and dark bands (an interference pattern) on the screen.
  • Particle — fire one photon at a time. Each lands as a single random dot. Click Fire one repeatedly.
  • Stream — fire rapidly. Thousands of random dots pile up into… the exact same striped pattern. Every particle somehow "knows" about both slits.

The mystery: the observer

Switch on the which-path detector to record which slit each particle goes through. The instant the path is known, the stripes vanish and the screen shows a single smooth blob. Stop watching, and the interference returns. Measurement itself changes the outcome.

Controls

  • Wavelength — color of the light. Shorter wavelength → tighter fringes.
  • Slit separation d — gap between slits. Wider d → tighter fringes.
  • Slit width a — how wide each slit is. Sets the broad brightness envelope.
  • Screen distance L — farther screen → wider-spaced fringes.
  • ⟲ Reset screen (top-right) — wipe the accumulated hits and start fresh.
  • The graph (bottom-right) overlays the theoretical intensity curve on your actual detected hits — watch them converge.

Navigation

  • Drag to orbit · scroll to zoom · the Wave field / Theory curve / Time chips toggle the ripple layer, the graph curve, and pause/play.
